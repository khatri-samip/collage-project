Boudhanath Stupa

One of the largest Buddhist stupas in Nepal, a spiritual center for Tibetan Buddhism and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Overview & Key Facts

Location: Kathmandu, Nepal

Type: Buddhist stupa

UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site since 1979

Significance: Major pilgrimage site for Tibetan Buddhists and symbol of enlightenment.

Established: 5th–6th century CE (approx.)

Historical & Cultural Significance

Boudhanath Stupa is one of the largest spherical stupas in Nepal. It has been a focal point for Tibetan Buddhism and pilgrimage. The stupa’s white dome topped with a gilded tower and Buddha’s eyes is iconic. Surrounding the stupa are monasteries, shops, and a community of Tibetan refugees.

Architecture & Features

  • The Dome: Represents the earth; massive circular base.
  • All-Seeing Eyes: Painted on the square tower above the dome; symbolize Buddha’s omniscience.
  • Prayer Wheels: Encircle the stupa; spinning them accumulates merit.
  • Monasteries & Gompas: Tibetan Buddhist monasteries surround the stupa.
  • Circular Walk: Pilgrims circumambulate the stupa clockwise while chanting mantras.

Festivals & Rituals

  • Buddha Jayanti: Celebrating Buddha’s birth, enlightenment, and death.
  • Losar (Tibetan New Year): Ceremonial prayers, dances, and offerings.
  • Daily Rituals: Monks and pilgrims perform pujas, light butter lamps, and circumambulate the stupa.

Visitor Information & Tips

  • Open year-round; best time: October–March.
  • Entry fee is minimal; donations support maintenance.
  • Dress modestly and respect religious customs.
  • Photography allowed but avoid disturbing pilgrims.
  • Walk clockwise around the stupa as a sign of respect.
