Chitwan National Park

Nepal’s first national park — a UNESCO World Heritage treasure famed for rhinos, tigers, and rich subtropical biodiversity.

Essential Facts & Rich Biodiversity

Area: 952.63 km² core area, 1,682 km² including buffer zones.

Status: UNESCO World Heritage Site (1984).

Mammals: Greater One-horned Rhinoceros, Royal Bengal Tiger, Sloth Bear, Asian Elephant, Leopard.

Birds: 544 species including Bengal Florican and migratory species.

Reptiles: Gharial, Mugger Crocodile, Asian Rock Python.

Geography & Climate

Location: Subtropical Terai lowlands of south-central Nepal.

Altitude: 100–815 m.

Climate: Tropical Monsoon; best to visit Oct–Feb.

Conservation History

Established in 1973 after poaching crisis; buffer zones and community-based programs protect wildlife.
