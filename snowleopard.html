Snow Leopard

The elusive Snow Leopard, often called the “Ghost of the Mountains,” roams the icy highlands of Nepal’s Himalayas.

About the Snow Leopard

The Snow Leopard (Panthera uncia) is a rare and magnificent predator adapted to life in the rugged mountains of Central and South Asia. Known for its thick fur, long tail, and piercing blue-grey eyes, it is one of Nepal’s most iconic and mysterious animals.

Habitat and Behavior

Snow leopards inhabit steep, rocky slopes at elevations between 3,000 to 6,000 meters. They are solitary, shy, and mostly nocturnal hunters, preying on blue sheep, Himalayan tahr, and marmots.

Where They Are Found in Nepal

In Nepal, snow leopards are found in the high Himalayas, including regions of Sagarmatha National Park, Annapurna Conservation Area, Shey Phoksundo National Park, and Manaslu Conservation Area. These areas are crucial for their survival and conservation.

Conservation Efforts

Conservation groups and the government of Nepal have made strong efforts to protect snow leopards from poaching and habitat loss. The Snow Leopard Conservation Program and community-based initiatives involve locals in protecting both the species and its environment.

Fun Facts

Snow leopards cannot roar like other big cats. Their long tails, which can be as long as their body, help them maintain balance on rocky cliffs. They are also excellent jumpers, capable of leaping up to 15 meters in a single bound!
