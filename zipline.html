Zipline in Nepal

Fly over valleys, rivers, and forests on some of the world’s longest and highest ziplines with breathtaking Himalayan views.

↓
Locations below

Overview

Nepal offers thrilling zipline experiences for adventure seekers. Soar high above rivers, gorges, and forests while enjoying panoramic Himalayan vistas. Ziplines vary in height, length, and speed, catering to beginners and thrill-seekers alike.

  • Zipline Levels: Beginner to extreme levels.
  • Popular Locations: The Last Resort (Bhote Koshi), Pokhara zipline, and Shivapuri Adventure Park.
  • Safety: Professional guides, harnesses, and helmets provided.
  • Nearby Activities: Rafting, bungee, trekking, and canyoning.

Zipline Tips

  • Follow all safety instructions and harness checks.
  • Wear comfortable clothing and closed shoes.
  • Keep your hands and arms inside the zipline harness.
  • Bring a waterproof camera if allowed.
  • Check weight and height restrictions before booking.

Best Time for Zipline

  • Spring (March–May): Clear skies and comfortable weather.
  • Autumn (Sept–Nov): Cool, perfect for adventure activities.
  • Winter (Dec–Feb): Fewer crowds, chilly but exhilarating rides.
  • Monsoon (June–Aug): Avoid due to slippery platforms and rain.


Popular Zipline Locations

The Last Resort - Bhote Koshi

One of the world’s highest ziplines, offering spectacular views of Bhote Koshi river gorge and surrounding mountains.

Pokhara Zipline

Longest zipline in Asia with 1.8 km of adrenaline-pumping ride across rivers, valleys, and hills with Annapurna range backdrop.

Shivapuri Adventure Park

Zipline adventure in the lush Shivapuri National Park, combining thrilling heights and scenic forested views.
