Paragliding in Nepal

Soar high above valleys, lakes, and the Himalayas — an adventure of a lifetime awaits you in the skies of Nepal.

↓
Popular paragliding sites below

Overview

Paragliding in Nepal offers stunning bird’s-eye views of the Himalayan peaks, serene lakes, and lush valleys. Pokhara is the world’s top destination, but other spots like Sirkot and Bandipur provide equally thrilling experiences. Whether you’re a beginner or an expert, Nepal’s thermals and weather conditions make it ideal for paragliding adventures.

Best Time for Paragliding

  • Autumn (Sept–Nov): Clear skies, perfect wind conditions.
  • Spring (Mar–May): Warm weather and excellent visibility.
  • Winter (Dec–Feb): Great for Pokhara — stable air and peaceful rides.


Popular Paragliding Places

Pokhara

The paragliding capital of Nepal. Glide above Phewa Lake with breathtaking views of Annapurna and Machhapuchhre mountains.

Sirkot

A quiet alternative to Pokhara, known for early morning flights above misty valleys and scenic ridges.

Bandipur

Fly over an ancient hilltop town with panoramic mountain views and tranquil countryside landscapes.

Kot Danda (Kathmandu)

Perfect for quick flights near the capital — enjoy bird’s-eye views of Kathmandu Valley and surrounding hills.

Dharan

Eastern Nepal’s paragliding site, offering flights over the lush hills and scenic plains below.
