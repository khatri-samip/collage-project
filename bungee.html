Bungee Jump in Nepal

Feel the ultimate adrenaline rush by bungee jumping off Nepal’s iconic bridges and cliffs with stunning Himalayan views.

↓
Locations below

Overview

Nepal is home to some of the most thrilling bungee jumping spots in the world. Jump off high bridges and cliffs surrounded by rivers, gorges, and mountains for a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

  • Bungee Levels: Suitable for beginners and adventure enthusiasts alike.
  • Popular Locations: The Last Resort (Bhote Koshi), Upper Trishuli, and suspension bridges across gorges.
  • Safety: Fully equipped with professional instructors and safety gear.
  • Adventure Activities Nearby: Rafting, zip-lining, canyoning, and trekking.

Bungee Jump Tips

  • Follow instructor safety instructions strictly.
  • Wear comfortable sportswear and secure shoes.
  • Remove loose items and accessories.
  • Check weight limits before jumping.
  • Bring a waterproof camera for memories.

Best Time for Bungee Jumping

  • Spring (March–May): Moderate weather, clear views of gorges and rivers.
  • Autumn (Sept–Nov): Cool weather, ideal for adventure activities.
  • Winter (Dec–Feb): Cold but fewer crowds; experience crisp mountain air.
  • Monsoon (June–Aug): Avoid due to high river levels and wet conditions.


Popular Bungee Jump Locations

The Last Resort - Bhote Koshi

World-famous bungee site, 160m above the Bhote Koshi river. Perfect for thrill-seekers and adventure lovers.

Kushma Bungee Jump

Kushma Bungee Jump is Nepal’s highest and the world’s second-highest bungee jump at a height of 228 meters. Located between the Baglung and Kushma districts, it offers breathtaking views of the Kaligandaki River Gorge. The 520-meter suspension bridge connects the two districts and serves as the jump platform. Prices start at Rs 8000 per person, with optional photos, videos, and T-shirts available for Rs 1500. Open 7 days a week, this thrilling jump can be reached within 2 hours from Pokhara.

Suspension Bridge Jumps

Smaller but thrilling jumps over suspension bridges in scenic river valleys, ideal for first-time jumpers.
