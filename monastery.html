Thrangu Monastery

One of Nepal's major Buddhist monasteries, located in Namo Buddha, a center for meditation and learning of Tibetan Buddhism.

Overview & Key Facts

Location: Namo Buddha, Kavrepalanchok District, Nepal

Type: Tibetan Buddhist Monastery

Significance: Spiritual center for study, meditation, and Tibetan Buddhist rituals.

Founded: Established in 1978 by the Thrangu Rinpoche lineage.

Historical & Cultural Importance

Thrangu Monastery is an important center for Tibetan Buddhism in Nepal. Namo Buddha is associated with the legend of the compassionate Bodhisattva who offered his body to feed a starving tigress, emphasizing altruism and wisdom. The monastery is an active center for religious teachings, retreats, and meditation practices.

Architecture & Features

  • Main Stupa: Houses sacred relics and represents the Buddha's mind.
  • Prayer Halls & Temples: Beautifully decorated with Tibetan murals and statues of Gautam Buddha.
  • Monastic School: Provides education and training for monks in Buddhist philosophy.
  • Retreat Centers: Facilities for meditation retreats for locals and international visitors.
  • Peaceful Gardens: Surrounding areas designed for reflection and mindfulness.
