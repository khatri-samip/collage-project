Pashupatinath Temple

The most sacred Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located on the banks of the Bagmati River in Kathmandu.

Overview & Key Facts

Location: Kathmandu, Bagmati Province, Nepal

Deity: Lord Shiva

Significance: UNESCO World Heritage Site, one of the holiest Hindu temples in Nepal and Asia

Architecture: Pagoda-style temple with intricate wood carvings and gilded roofs

Festivals: Maha Shivaratri is the biggest celebration attracting thousands of devotees

Historical & Cultural Significance

Pashupatinath Temple is believed to have existed since 400 A.D., although the current pagoda structure was built in the 17th century. It is the spiritual heart of Nepalese Hindus and a key pilgrimage site. The temple's sacred Bagmati River is used for ritual bathing and cremation ceremonies along its ghats.

Architecture & Temple Complex

  • Main temple: two-story pagoda with gilded roof and silver doors.
  • Inner sanctum houses a stone idol of Pashupati, Shiva as “Lord of Animals.”
  • Temple complex along Bagmati River with smaller shrines.
  • Wooden carvings, statues, and traditional Hindu motifs.
  • Ashrams, dharmashalas, and shelters for sadhus.

Rituals & Ceremonies

  • Daily aarti at sunrise and sunset.
  • Maha Shivaratri festival: largest religious gathering in Nepal.
  • Cremation ceremonies on the ghats along Bagmati River.
  • Offerings: flowers, milk, holy water, Bilva leaves.
  • Witness sadhus meditating and chanting hymns.

Visiting & Logistics

  • Open year-round; early morning or late afternoon recommended.
  • Entry fee for non-Hindus at main temple entrance.
  • Dress code: shoulders and knees covered inside temple complex.
  • Nearby attractions: Ghats, Bishnumati River confluence, Kumbheshwar Temple, local markets.
  • Best time: October–March; Maha Shivaratri in Feb/March is special.

Nearby Attractions & Experiences

  • Bagmati River ghats: observe cremation ceremonies.
  • Sadhus & Holy Men: learn about ascetic life.
  • Kathmandu Durbar Square: UNESCO site with palaces, temples, museums.
  • Patan and Bhaktapur: explore ancient cities with Newar culture.
  • Local markets: handicrafts, prayer beads, religious souvenirs.

Visitor Tips

  • Respect religious practices; do not touch idols or sacred items.
  • Photography restricted inside main sanctum; ask permission for sadhus.
  • Carry small cash for donations.
  • Wear comfortable footwear; stone paths involved.
  • Hire local guide for history and rituals.
