Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions? We’ve got answers. Below are some of the most common questions travelers ask about visiting Nepal and using Discover Nepal.

Do I need a visa to visit Nepal?
Yes, most travelers need a visa to enter Nepal. You can obtain a visa on arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport or apply online through the Nepal Immigration website. Ensure your passport is valid for at least six months beyond your travel dates.
What is the best time to visit Nepal?
The best times to visit Nepal are during spring (March–May) and autumn (September–November). During these months, the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear—perfect for trekking and sightseeing.
Is Nepal safe for solo travelers?
Nepal is generally very safe for solo travelers. The locals are kind and welcoming. However, always take basic safety precautions—avoid isolated areas at night and keep valuables secure.
Do I need travel insurance before visiting Nepal?
Yes, travel insurance is strongly recommended, especially if you plan to trek or do adventure sports. It should cover medical emergencies, cancellations, and evacuation.
What currency is used in Nepal?
The Nepalese Rupee (NPR) is the official currency. You can exchange foreign currency at banks and authorized exchange counters. Cash is preferred in rural areas.
Is internet access available throughout Nepal?
Yes, Wi-Fi is available in most cities and hotels. For remote trekking routes, it’s advisable to get a local SIM card with data for reliable connectivity.
What should I pack for my Nepal trip?
Pack according to the season and your activities. Essentials include layered clothing, trekking shoes, sunscreen, reusable bottles, and basic first aid items.
How can I contact Discover Nepal for assistance?
You can reach us through our Contact Page. We reply within 24 hours for travel queries, bookings, or general assistance.
Are trekking permits required in Nepal?
Yes, most trekking routes require permits such as the TIMS card and conservation area permits (like for Annapurna or Everest regions). We can guide you on obtaining these easily.
Can I use credit cards in Nepal?
Credit cards are accepted in major hotels, restaurants, and shops in Kathmandu and Pokhara. However, smaller towns and trekking areas prefer cash payments.
What languages are spoken in Nepal?
Nepali is the official language, but English is widely understood in tourist areas. Learning a few local greetings will earn you warm smiles and better connections.
Is it safe to drink tap water in Nepal?
Tap water is not safe to drink. Always use bottled or filtered water. We recommend carrying a reusable filter bottle to stay eco-friendly.
How reliable is transportation in Nepal?
Domestic flights, buses, and taxis are available across the country. Mountain weather can affect flight schedules, so plan flexible travel days.
Can I trek without a guide?
While some routes are open to solo trekkers, hiring a guide is recommended for safety, navigation, and cultural insight—especially in remote regions.
Do I need vaccinations before traveling to Nepal?
Yes, consult your doctor about vaccines for Hepatitis A, Typhoid, and Tetanus. If you’re visiting rural areas, discuss malaria precautions too.
What are some cultural etiquette tips?
Remove shoes before entering homes or temples, dress modestly, and avoid public displays of affection. A slight bow with palms together (“Namaste”) is a polite greeting.
What emergency numbers should I know?
The emergency numbers in Nepal are: Police – 100, Ambulance – 102, Fire – 101. Keep your embassy’s contact info handy as well.
Are there eco-friendly travel options in Nepal?
Yes, you can choose eco-lodges, support local guides, and avoid plastic waste. Many tour operators now follow sustainable and responsible tourism practices.
